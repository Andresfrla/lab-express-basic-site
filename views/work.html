About his Work

Bruce Dickinson's work encompasses a wide range of creative endeavors, including his contributions as a musician, songwriter, vocalist, pilot, author, and broadcaster. Here are some highlights of his diverse body of work: Music with Iron Maiden: Bruce Dickinson joined Iron Maiden as their lead vocalist in 1981, replacing Paul Di'Anno. His soaring vocal range, dynamic stage presence, and songwriting abilities played a significant role in the band's success. Dickinson recorded some of Iron Maiden's most iconic albums, including "The Number of the Beast" (1982), "Powerslave" (1984), "Seventh Son of a Seventh Son" (1988), and "Brave New World" (2000). He left the band in 1993 but returned in 1999 and continues to tour and record with Iron Maiden. Solo Music Career: Alongside his work with Iron Maiden, Bruce Dickinson has released several solo albums. His solo material explores a broader range of musical styles, incorporating elements of hard rock, progressive rock, and alternative rock. Notable albums include "Tattooed Millionaire" (1990), "The Chemical Wedding" (1998), and "Tyranny of Souls" (2005). Aviation Career: Bruce Dickinson has had a long-standing passion for aviation. He obtained his pilot's license in the 1990s and went on to fly commercial aircraft for the British airline Astraeus. Dickinson has piloted Boeing 757 and 737 aircraft, using his time off from music to pursue his love for flying. Authorship: In 2017, Bruce Dickinson published his autobiography titled "What Does This Button Do?," which became a bestseller. The book provides a personal account of his life and career, offering insights into his experiences in the music industry, his passion for aviation, and his various adventures. Broadcaster and Presenter: Dickinson has also ventured into broadcasting. He has hosted his radio shows, such as "Friday Rock Show" on BBC Radio 6 Music, where he shares his knowledge and passion for music with listeners. He has also appeared as a guest on various television programs, sharing his expertise and experiences. Bruce Dickinson's work reflects his versatility as an artist, showcasing his immense talent and passion in different fields. Whether it's his powerful vocals with Iron Maiden, his exploration of various musical styles in his solo work, his piloting endeavors, or his literary contributions, Dickinson has left an indelible mark on multiple industries, earning him a well-deserved reputation as a highly accomplished and influential figure.
